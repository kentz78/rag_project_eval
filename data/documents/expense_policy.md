# Expense and Travel Policy — Acme Robotics Pte Ltd

## 1. General Principle

All business expenses must be reasonable, necessary, and directly related to company business. Employees should exercise the same judgment spending company money as they would their own.

## 2. Claim Submission

Expense claims must be submitted via the finance portal within 30 days of the expense being incurred, accompanied by an itemised receipt. Claims submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed except in exceptional circumstances approved by Finance.

## 3. Approval Limits

- Claims up to SGD 200 require approval from the direct manager only.
- Claims from SGD 200 to SGD 1,000 require approval from the direct manager and the department head.
- Claims above SGD 1,000 require additional approval from Finance.

## 4. Travel

Economy class is standard for flights under 6 hours; premium economy is permitted for flights over 6 hours. Business class requires VP-level approval. Hotel accommodation should not exceed SGD 250 per night in most cities, or SGD 400 per night in high-cost cities (e.g., Tokyo, New York, London, Zurich) — see the Finance intranet page for the full high-cost city list.

## 5. Meals

Meal per diem while travelling on business is SGD 80 per day, covering breakfast, lunch, and dinner combined, and does not require individual itemised receipts (a credit card statement or single consolidated receipt suffices).

## 6. Non-Reimbursable Items

The company will not reimburse: alcohol not part of a client entertainment event, traffic or parking fines, personal entertainment, gym or spa charges, or upgrades to business/first class without prior approval.

## 7. Client Entertainment

Client entertainment expenses require the names of attendees and the business purpose to be recorded on the claim. Client entertainment spend per person per event should not exceed SGD 150 without prior manager approval.
